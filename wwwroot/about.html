Our story

One thread pitch changed
how we think about bottles.

HydraStack started with a drawer full of almost-right water bottles — and one stubborn question: why should you buy a whole new bottle when only one part of it is wrong?

The drawer problem

Everyone has the drawer. The bottle with the perfect straw lid but a body that never fits a cupholder. The insulated one that keeps coffee hot but is too big for the gym. The slim one you love that holds barely two glasses of water. Each bottle is right for one hour of your day and wrong for the rest of it.

We kept asking why bottles are sold as monoliths. Your day is modular — commute, desk, gym, trail — but your bottle isn't. So we started machining threads.

The tube principle

The answer turned out to be almost embarrassingly simple: give every module the same thread pitch. If the lid screws onto the mid, and the mid screws onto the base with the exact same thread, every part becomes swappable. Buy the chamber once. Swap the lid for how you drink. Swap the base for where you're going.

That single decision — one universal thread — is the entire company. Everything else is just good manufacturing.

Buy once, swap forever

A HydraStack isn't a product you replace — it's a system you extend. Lose a lid? Replace the lid. Want heat? Add the heated base. The chamber you already own stays.

Less landfill, more refill

Modularity is quietly a sustainability play: replacing one worn part instead of a whole bottle keeps steel in use and out of the bin.

Milestones

How we got here

Late 2024

The sketch

Three rectangles and a note: "same thread everywhere?" The first prototype was two store-bought bottles and a 3D-printed adapter that leaked heroically.

Spring 2025

The thread that held

Seventeen thread profiles later, one sealed on the first try and kept sealing after a thousand swap cycles. That pitch is now cut into every module we make.

Winter 2025

The heated base

The module that proved the system: a rechargeable warming element that threads onto any mid — including the very first chamber we ever machined.

Today

Your build

Three lids, three mids, four bases, six colorways, and a community sharing builds we never imagined. The drawer problem, retired.
